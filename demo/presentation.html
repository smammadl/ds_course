Tools of the Trade: Languages

  • Python: Default language for Data Science right now.
  • SQL: Essential for talking to databases.
  • R: Language for statistical analysis.
If you want to start today, learn Python and SQL. Python is readable, versatile, and has the biggest ecosystem. SQL has been around since the 1970s and is still the most reliable way to pull data out of a company's database.

Tools of the Trade: Libraries

  • Data Manipulation: Pandas, NumPy
  • Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn
  • Machine Learning: Scikit-Learn, PyTorch
built-in algorithms, Pandas = Excel

Tools of the Trade: Math & Statistics

  • Linear Algebra: Vectors, Matrices
  • Calculus: Functions, Derivatives
  • Statistics: Mean, Median, Mode, Standard Deviation, Variance, Correlation, Probability.
Math & Statistics are the foundation of Data Science. Without a strong understanding of math and statistics, you will not be able to build effective models.

Current Job Market & Opportunities

The "Sexiest Job of the 21st Century"

  • Low cost of entry
  • Massive data generation = Massive need for analysts
  • High ROI for companies
  • Adjacencies: Data Analyst, Data Engineer, Machine Learning Engineer, Backend Engineer, Frontend Engineer, etc.
keywords: low cost of entry, massive data generation, high ROI, adjacencies.

Current Job Market & Risks

  • Intense competition
  • Rapid obsolescence of skills
  • Automation of basic tasks
  • Reality Gap
keywords: intense competition, rapid obsolescence of skills.

Future Prospects

  • Generative AI (LLMs): Automating code writing, but requiring human oversight.
  • AutoML: The basic algorithms will be automated.
  • The Pivot: Future Data Scientists will need have diverse skills, not just coders.
Will AI take Data Science jobs? AI will automate the boring parts of Data Science. AutoML can test 50 algorithms in a minute. The future belongs to the Data Scientist who can look at a messy business problem, figure out how to translate it into math, and then explain the results to a CEO who doesn't know what a neural network is.
